Evolution of mineralising cells
in chondrichthyans

How proteins shape teeth has been extensively investigated in mammals. In contrast, the role of homologous proteins in elasmobranchs remains cryptic. In this project, we investigate the role of Shh, Fgf3, and Bmp4 in generating intra- and interspecific tooth shape diversity in sharks. We perform 1) functional tests by implanting micro beads soaked with proteins in the dental lamina of catsharks and 2) mimic the tooth phenotypes generated with tooth modelling.

Methods: In vivo: Micro bead insertion; In silico: modelling of tooth growth

Related publication:

R. Zimm, F. Berio, M. Debiais-Thibaud, & N. Goudemand (Under review). A shark-inspired general model of tooth morphogenesis unveils developmental asymmetries in phenotype transitions. — PNAS. https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2216959120
