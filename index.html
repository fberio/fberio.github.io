About
me

I have a Master degree in marine ecology and I am currently doing my PhD in evo-devo on elasmobranch fishes, especially on catsharks. I try to describe their tooth shape diversity using microCT scans and 3D geometric morphometrics. Then, I am deciphering what drives such diversity, including physical and molecular parameters. I work under the co-supervision of Nicolas Goudemand and Mélanie Debiais-Thibaud. I also worked on skeletal patterns in skates at the University of Québec in Rimouski in the Paléobio lab. Then, although it is not part of my current research I have been and I am still highly interested in marine ecology and conservation.
